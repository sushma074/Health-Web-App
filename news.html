AI is increasingly being used to predict the progression of autoimmune diseases, providing valuable insights for early intervention and personalized treatment plans. By analyzing vast datasets from patient medical histories, genetic information, and biomarkers, AI algorithms identify patterns that human analysis might miss.

Less Doctors

India is facing huge Shortage of Doctors. The shortage of professionalslike doctors, nurses, technicians and infrastructures as given by the report of WHO that 0.76 doctors and 2.09 nurses are present per 1000 people. Additionally, Indian healthcare is also facing acute shortage of hospital beds with 1.3 hospital beds per 1000 population.To address this shortage, the Indian government plans to establish approximately 200 new medical colleges over the next decade, aiming to meet the projected need for an additional 600,000 doctors

Good Healthcare Facilities

Access to good healthcare facilities remains a significant challenge for the common people in India, despite advancements in medical technology and healthcare infrastructure. While urban areas boast high-quality hospitals and specialized care centers, rural and economically disadvantaged regions face severe shortages of medical professionals, basic equipment, and modern healthcare services. The cost of private healthcare is prohibitively expensive for many, pushing a large segment of the population toward overcrowded and underfunded public hospitals. Issues like delayed diagnoses, inadequate emergency care, and a lack of preventive health services exacerbate the situation.
